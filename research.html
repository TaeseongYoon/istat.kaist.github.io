iStat Lab’s primary research goal is to advance statistical and machine learning methodologies, with a focus on addressing complex real-world challenges and improving the quality of industrial systems.

Specific Research Topics

1. Time-Series Analysis

We study high-dimensional multivariate time-series data generated from industrial systems, aiming to detect anomalies and forecast future behavior. Our research focuses on:

  • Multivariate Time-Series Anomaly Detection: Identifying abnormal patterns in complex sensor networks.
  • Spatiotemporal Forecasting: Modeling temporal and spatial dependencies for process-level prediction.

2. Uncertainty Quantification (UQ) in Deep Learning

Reliable AI requires models that understand and express uncertainty. We design learning frameworks that quantify both prediction confidence and data uncertainty:

  • Evidential Deep Learning: Learning both predictions and uncertainties from data in a single forward pass.
  • Bayesian Deep Learning: Estimating uncertainty by placing distributions over model parameters.
  • Conformal Prediction: Providing statistically guaranteed prediction sets with controlled coverage.

3. Learning from Incomplete and Imbalanced Data

Real-world industrial data are often imperfect—containing missing labels, noisy samples, or class imbalance. We develop algorithms that remain robust and generalizable under such data imperfections:

  • Class-Imbalanced Semi-Supervised Learning: Combining label efficiency and fairness across classes.
  • Noisy Label Learning: Training reliable models even when annotation errors exist.

4. Causal Inference and Representation Learning

Understanding causality is crucial for actionable decision-making. We study causal reasoning and representation learning to uncover true relationships within data:

  • Causal Inference: Discovering causal relationships from observational data.
  • Causal Representation Learning: Learning latent features that reflect underlying causal structures.
